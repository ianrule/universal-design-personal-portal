Universal Design How-To

It is best to view the Universal Design as a design mindset rather than a set-in-stone guideline to follow. Although there are many guidelines and checklists available online to aid designers in adoption of Universal Design, UD best practices will continue to evolve with new technology and a deeper understanding of the spectrum of human ability. My recommendation to any designer who wishes to learn more about the application of Universal Design is this: research the accessible practices currently available in your realm of design prior to jumping into the design process. For example, web designers can reference the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines as a start in making their websites a more inclusive space. Making Universal Design a priority at the beginning of your design process will save you more work in the long run as the demographic profile of our society shifts and legislation requires adherence to accessibility rules and regulations. Strive for progress in creating designs that are more inclusive, stay open to user feedback, and you are headed in the right direction. A beginner guideline in making your designs more universal can be found in the Centre for Excellence in Universal Design's article on the 7 Principles of Universal Design. For web developers, a quick start guide to making websites more universally accessible is provided in an aside on this page. Thank you for embarking on this educational journey today, and I hope you feel inspired to join us in the Universal Design movement!
